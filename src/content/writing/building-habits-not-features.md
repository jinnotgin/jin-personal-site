We talk about features because they are easy to point at. A feature has a name, a launch, a changelog line. It is a noun. You can put it on a roadmap and feel like the roadmap is doing something.

A habit has none of that. It is a verb, and a quiet one. Nobody screenshots a habit.

But the systems that matter are the ones people return to without being asked. The feature is the thing you build. The habit is the thing that decides whether the building was worth it.

## The gap between shipped and used

Most "we shipped it and nobody used it" stories are not adoption problems. They are habit problems wearing an adoption costume. The feature worked. It just never found a slot in anyone's day where it was the obvious next move.

A useful question to ask before building: what does this replace, and is the thing it replaces worse on a Tuesday afternoon when nobody is paying attention? If the honest answer is "about the same, but ours is newer", the habit will not form. Novelty is not a slot.

## What I do differently now

Three changes, none of them clever:

- **Design for the second use, not the first.** The demo is the first use and it always works. The second use is where the habit lives or dies, and it usually dies because the setup cost was front-loaded and the payoff was not.
- **Find the existing habit and sit next to it.** New habits are expensive. Adjacent ones are cheap. The tools that stuck in my own work attached themselves to something I already did every day.
- **Instrument return, not reach.** Reach tells you the launch worked. Return tells you the habit is forming. Only one of those compounds.

> A feature ships once. A habit compounds. Most of the disappointment in product work is the distance between those two sentences.

This is also why I am suspicious of metrics that go up at launch and then get quietly stopped being reported. The graph that matters is boring, slow, and still being watched six months later.

None of this is an argument against shipping. It is an argument for being honest about what shipping was for.
