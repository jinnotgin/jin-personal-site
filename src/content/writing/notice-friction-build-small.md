If you line up the projects in the Workbench, most of them are the same move repeated. Notice a friction that everyone has quietly accepted. Build the smallest system that removes it. Stop there.

Now In Singapore was that. FavEats was that. Nearly was that. The Smart Mirror was that, in hardware.

## Why small

Small is not modesty. Small is a strategy.

- A small system can be honest about what it does not do.
- A small system has a legible failure mode. You can hold the whole thing in your head, so when it breaks you know why.
- A small system survives losing interest. Half of these are archived, and they still work, because there was not much to rot.

The temptation is always to generalise: the reminder app could be a whole productivity suite, the civic feed could be a platform. Every time I followed that temptation the project died of its own ambition before it helped anyone.

The friction was specific. The fix should be too.
