# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The site is for recruiters, future collaborators, people considering working with Jin, and people who look him up to judge credibility, judgment, and point of view. They may arrive with little context, often from LinkedIn, GitHub, search, or a shared link, and need to understand both professional substance and personal signal quickly.

## Product Purpose

This is Jin's personal brand, portfolio, and writing home. It should explain what he does, how he thinks, what he has built, and what shaped his journey without becoming a resume dump. Success means a visitor can understand Jin's credibility within 60 seconds, then keep exploring his writing, projects, learning trails, and career story if they want more depth.

## Brand Personality

Practical, systems-minded, humane, curious, and quietly playful. Jin works between emerging technology, organisational change, and human systems: turning ambiguous possibilities into useful practice. The site should feel credible enough for senior stakeholders, direct enough for builders, and personal enough that it feels like a real person made it.

Public-facing naming should usually be "Jin." Use "Lin Jin" where formal identity or credentials need it.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic portfolio hero sections, inflated AI/innovation language, corporate resume templates, and polished-but-empty personal branding. Avoid a site that feels like LinkedIn reformatted into cards. Avoid over-serious consultant aesthetics, shallow futurism, and decorative tech motifs that do not reveal how Jin actually thinks or works.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with plain credibility: state the work and stakes directly before adding personality.
2. Show systems through structure: make the site itself feel like a thoughtful map of Jin's work, learning, writing, and influences.
3. Balance human and technical: pair applied AI, product, and platform work with the practices and values that make teams healthier.
4. Reward exploration: let visitors skim quickly, but give curious people categorized paths into writing, projects, references, and personal notes.
5. Keep the maker signal visible: include evidence of prototyping, DIY energy, GitHub work, and experiments without making the site feel chaotic.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Prioritize readable typography, strong contrast, keyboard navigation, reduced-motion support, and content that remains coherent without animation. The site should work well for quick scanning on mobile and desktop, and should not rely on color alone to communicate categories or status.
