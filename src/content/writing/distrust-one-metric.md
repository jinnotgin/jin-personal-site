CoinGossip existed because everyone was watching price and price is a lagging, crowded signal. Attention moved first. Almost nobody measured attention, because it was harder and less satisfying than a chart that goes up and down.

The lesson generalised well beyond crypto.

A single metric is a story the organisation tells itself. It is usually the metric that is easiest to collect, not the one that is most honest. The moment a number becomes the number, it stops describing the system and starts being gamed by it.

What I do now, on anything that matters:

- Pair every headline metric with the metric that would embarrass it.
- Watch the leading, noisy signal even when the lagging, clean one is more comfortable to report.
- Treat a metric that suddenly stops being reported as data. It is usually the most informative event in the chart.

Show the workings, not just the conclusion. A dashboard with one confident number is a dashboard hiding something.
